# Product and Technical Roadmap

## Purpose

This document defines how AI Film Studio plans, prioritizes, communicates, and reviews product and technical roadmap work.

## Objectives

- Align product development with business goals.
- Balance customer value, reliability, security, and technical sustainability.
- Make priorities and tradeoffs visible.
- Coordinate dependencies across teams.
- Keep roadmap commitments realistic and reviewable.

## Roadmap Horizons

### Near Term

Work expected within the next delivery cycle or quarter.

Near-term items should have:

- Clear owner
- Defined outcome
- Approved scope
- Known dependencies
- Delivery risks
- Acceptance criteria

### Medium Term

Work likely to be addressed within the following two to four quarters.

Medium-term items should describe intended outcomes without implying fixed delivery commitments unless formally approved.

### Long Term

Strategic opportunities, architectural direction, and exploratory initiatives beyond the current planning horizon.

Long-term items are directional and may change as evidence, constraints, or priorities evolve.

## Roadmap Categories

Roadmap items may include:

- Product capabilities
- User experience improvements
- Platform and infrastructure work
- Reliability improvements
- Security initiatives
- Performance and scalability
- Data and analytics
- Developer experience
- Compliance and governance
- Technical debt reduction
- Research and experimentation

## Prioritization Criteria

Roadmap decisions should consider:

- Customer and user impact
- Strategic alignment
- Security and compliance risk
- Reliability and operational risk
- Revenue or cost impact
- Engineering effort
- Dependencies
- Time sensitivity
- Reversibility
- Evidence and confidence level

## Required Item Metadata

Each roadmap item should include:

- Title
- Desired outcome
- Category
- Owner
- Target horizon
- Status
- Priority
- Dependencies
- Key risks
- Success measures
- Relevant architecture or design references

## Status Values

Use the following status values:

- Proposed
- Discovery
- Planned
- In Progress
- Blocked
- Delivered
- Deferred
- Cancelled

## Planning Process

1. Collect candidate initiatives from stakeholders, customer feedback, incidents, metrics, and technical reviews.
2. Define the problem and intended outcome.
3. Estimate scope, dependencies, and risk.
4. Prioritize against current goals and constraints.
5. Assign an owner and planning horizon.
6. Review roadmap changes with relevant stakeholders.
7. Track delivery and update status.
8. Measure outcomes after release.

## Technical Roadmap

The technical roadmap should explicitly account for:

- Architecture evolution
- Capacity requirements
- Dependency upgrades
- End-of-life technologies
- Security hardening
- Observability improvements
- Disaster recovery maturity
- Performance bottlenecks
- Technical debt
- Platform automation

## Change Management

Roadmap changes are expected, but material changes should document:

- What changed
- Why it changed
- Impact on other commitments
- New risks or dependencies
- Stakeholders notified

## Communication

Roadmap communication should:

- Distinguish committed work from directional work
- Avoid presenting uncertain dates as guarantees
- Explain major tradeoffs
- Highlight blocked or at-risk items
- Preserve confidentiality where required
- Use consistent status definitions

## Review Cadence

Recommended cadence:

- Near-term delivery review: weekly
- Roadmap health review: monthly
- Strategic prioritization review: quarterly
- Full roadmap refresh: at least annually

## Success Measurement

Delivered roadmap items should be evaluated against their intended outcomes using relevant measures such as:

- User adoption
- Task completion
- Reliability
- Performance
- Security risk reduction
- Operational efficiency
- Cost reduction
- Developer productivity
- Customer satisfaction
